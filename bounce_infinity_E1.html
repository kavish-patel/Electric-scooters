BOUNCE INFINITY E1

The Bounce Infinity E1 retails at Rs 68,999 for the version with swappable battery pack and charger. The one without battery pack relies on the company’s upcoming swap stations, and it is priced at Rs 45,099. Both ex-showroom Delhi.Bookings are open for a refundable Rs 499, and deliveries are slated to commence from March 2022. The electric scooter is available in Sporty Red, Sparkle Black, Pearl White, Desat Silver and Comed Grey.

The Bounce Infinity comes with an all-LED lighting system and a full digital instrument cluster with smartphone compatibility. Using a dedicated app, you can access features like geo-fencing, theft and tow detection, remote tracking, and battery charge status. There’s also 12-litre underseat storage with a USB charging port and a utility hook on the apron and below the seat for extra practicality. The electric scooter features a reverse mode, crawl function (can be used to crawl the scooter at walking speeds in case of a puncture), and cruise control.

The Bounce Infinity E1 is powered by a Brushless DC hub motor producing 83Nm. It propels the scooter to a claimed top speed of 65kmph, and 0-40kmph comes up in 8 seconds. The motor is linked to a 39Ah removable lithium-ion battery pack offering a claimed range of 85km. It takes around 4-5 hours to charge using a conventional power socket.

The Bounce Infinity E1 is built on a tubular frame with a telescopic front fork and twin rear shock absorbers. Braking is via disc at both ends with a regenerative braking system. The scooter rolls on alloy wheels wrapped with tubeless tyres.

Notable rivals of the Bounce Infinity E1 electric scooter include Okinawa iPraise Plus, BGauss B8, and Ampere Magnus Pro. If you’re looking for other alternatives to the variant without the battery pack, then you can check out the Bajaj CT100, Hero HF 100, and TVS XL100.
